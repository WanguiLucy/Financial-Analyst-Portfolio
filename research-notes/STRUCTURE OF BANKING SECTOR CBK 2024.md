## 1. What the Kenyan Banking Sector Looks Like (as at December 31, 2024)

The sector is bigger than just banks. It includes:
- 38 Commercial Banks + 1 Mortgage Finance Company = **39 banking institutions**
- 14 Microfinance Banks (MFBs)
- 85 Digital Credit Providers (DCPs) — the largest single category
- 26 Money Remittance Providers (MRPs)
- 81 Forex Bureaus
- 3 Credit Reference Bureaus (CRBs)
- 10 Representative Offices of foreign banks

**Ownership split:**
- 2 government-owned banks (minority presence)
- 20 locally owned private banks
- 17 foreign-owned banks (14 subsidiaries + 3 branches)

> **Why this matters for advisory work:** When a Big 4 client says "the banking sector," they mean all of this — not just commercial banks. Understanding the full ecosystem is foundational.

---

## 2. Who Controls the Assets

Total banking sector net assets: **Ksh. 7.6 trillion (2024)** — down slightly from Ksh. 7.7 trillion in 2023 (-1.6%).

| Ownership | No. of Banks | Share of Total Assets |
|---|---|---|
| Local Private Banks | 20 | 69.4% |
| Foreign Banks | 17 | 30.1% |
| Local Public Banks | 2 | 0.5% |

**Key insight:** 17 foreign banks control 30% of assets. 2 government banks control less than 1%. The private sector dominates entirely.

---

## 3. The Peer Group System — How CBK Classifies Banks

CBK ranks banks using a **weighted composite index** based on: net assets, deposits, capital & reserves, deposit accounts, and loan accounts.

| Peer Group | Threshold | No. of Banks (2024) | Combined Market Share |
|---|---|---|---|
| Large | ≥ 5% index | 9 | 75.6% |
| Medium | 1–5% index | 9 | 16.7% |
| Small | < 1% index | 21 | 7.7% |

**9 banks control 75.6% of the entire market.** 21 small banks share just 7.7%.

Notable 2024 movement: **HFC Ltd** moved from Small → Medium peer group.

Profitability: Sector profit before tax grew **18.2%** to Ksh. 260 billion in 2024.  
Large banks captured **89.3%** of all pre-tax profits.

> **Advisory angle:** Concentration risk is real. If 2–3 large banks had problems, the entire sector would be affected. This is why CBK supervision is so intensive for large peer group banks.

---

## 4. Branch Network and Access

- Total branches: **1,573** in 2024, up from 1,511 in 2023 (+62 branches, +4.1%)
- Kiambu County had the highest growth (+9 branches)
- ATMs barely grew: 2,282 → 2,289 (+7 ATMs, +0.31%)

**ATM growth is essentially flat** — banks are expanding branches but not ATMs. Suggests digital channels (mobile banking) are absorbing what ATMs used to do.

---

## 5. Microfinance Banks — A Sector Under Stress

MFBs are struggling compared to commercial banks:

- Total MFB assets: **Ksh. 57.9 billion** — down 9.8% from Ksh. 64.2 billion in 2023
- Net advances fell **16.8%** (Ksh. 37.5B → Ksh. 31.2B) — banks deliberately cutting lending to manage bad loans
- Customer deposits fell 1.9%
- Borrowings fell sharply — from Ksh. 9.2B to Ksh. 5.7B (-38%)
- **2 small MFBs now have negative market share** (negative equity — technically insolvent)

Top MFB: **Faulu MFB** (35.7% market share) — dominant leader.  
**Kenya Women MFB** dropped sharply from 29.1% → 12.1% market share.

> **Advisory angle:** Deteriorating MFB health signals financial inclusion challenges. Low-income borrowers who rely on MFBs are being squeezed out as institutions reduce lending. This is a systemic risk worth flagging in any advisory presentation on Kenya's financial sector.

---

## 6. Forex Bureaus and Money Remittances

- Forex bureaus: grew from 73 → **81** in 2024
- **87.7% of forex bureau outlets are in Nairobi** — extreme geographic concentration
- MRP outlets grew from 60 → 72
- MRP agents fell from 52 → 34 — customers shifting to **digital remittance channels** over cash pick-up

---

## My 3 Key Takeaways from Chapter 1

1. **Concentration is the defining feature of Kenya's banking sector.** 9 large banks = 75.6% of assets and 89.3% of profits. This shapes everything — regulation, risk, advisory work.

2. **Microfinance banks are in distress.** A 9.8% asset decline and two institutions with negative equity signals a sector that needs structural intervention, not just supervision.

3. **Digital is replacing physical infrastructure.** ATM growth is flat, MRP agents are declining — customers are moving to mobile and digital channels faster than institutions are building physical presence.
